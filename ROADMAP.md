# ROADMAP

## Skills Map

**Theoretical knowledge:**
History of philosophy (Ancient → Early Modern → 19th & 20th C.), metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, political theory, logic, philosophy of mind, philosophy of science, philosophy of language.

**Methods:**
Argument reconstruction, conceptual engineering, formal logic, thought experiments, textual exegesis, comparative analysis.

**Tools:**
Symbolic logic notation, essay-writing frameworks, citation practice, critical reading techniques.

**Practice:**
Regular writing assignments, problem sets in logic, participation in MOOC forums, self-assessment via past exams, notes using spaced repetition.

## Program Roadmap (1–2 Years, ~20 hr/week)

Below is one possible sequencing that keeps weekly workload sane.

### Phase 1 (3–4 months)

* Introduction to Philosophy
* Justice
* Intro Logic
* Moralities of Everyday Life

### Phase 2 (3–4 months)

* Ancient Greek Philosophy
* Early Modern Philosophy (Yale)
* Ethics (MIT)
* Logic: Language & Information (Stanford)

### Phase 3 (3–4 months)

* Epistemology (MIT)
* Metaphysics (MIT)
* Introduction to Political Philosophy (Yale)

### Phase 4 (3–4 months)

* Philosophy of Mind (MIT)
* Philosophy of Language (MIT)
* Philosophy of Science (Penn)

### Final Phase

* Capstone Project (2–3 months)
